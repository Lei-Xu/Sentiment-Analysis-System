It has superintending and reforming power over all decisions affecting activities held at the University or connected with the University. It is composed of 25 voting members and one non-voting observer, the breakdown of which is outlined in article 25 of the By-Laws. Its general powers are set out in the University Charter while its specific functions and powers are listed under article 41 of the By-Laws.\n\xa0\nThe Chair of the Board of Governors chairs all meetings of the Board and of the Corporation. He/she is an Officer of the University and an ex-officio member of the Board of Governors, the majority of its standing committees as well as the Corporation.\nOn July 1, 2015, Mr. Norman E. Hébert jr. began his second three-year term as Chair of Board of Governors.\xa0 Mr. Hébert was first appointed to the Board on July 1, 2008 and served as Board Vice-Chair from December 8, 2011 to June 30, 2012.\nMr. Hébert has been president and chief executive officer of Groupe Park Avenue Inc., a family company and one of Canada’s first multi-franchise automobile dealership operations, for more than 20 years.\nHe served for six years as a member of the Advisory Board of Concordia's John Molson School of Business.\xa0 He is also the founder of Concordia’s annual golf tournament, which raised funds for student bursaries.\nMr. Hébert\xa0has a Commerce degree from Concordia University and Law degree from the University of Ottawa. \xa0He is also a member of the Quebec Bar.\n\nThroughout his career, Mr. Hébert has received many prestigious awards and accolades.\xa0 He has served on several boards, including Hydro-Québec and Société des alcools du Québec which he chaired from 2006 to 2013. As well, he has served and currently serves on several boards in the automotive industry and charitable organizations, such as Centraide of Greater Montreal and the Hôpital Charles-Lemoyne Foundation.\n* deceased\nThe Chancellor is the titular head of the University and serves as an ambassador to the University. The Chancellor presides at convocations and similar ceremonies and performs such official duties as the Board of Governors may designate. In accordance with the By-Laws, the Chancellor is a non-voting observer on the Board of Governors, a member of the Corporation and an Officer of the University.\nOn June 9, 2017, the Board of Governors appointed Jonathan Wener, C.M. as Chancellor of Concordia University, for a second three-year term beginning on January 1, 2018. Prior to his appointment as Chancellor, Mr. Wener served as Deputy Chancellor.\nPrior to his appointment as Deputy Chancellor, Mr. Wener served on the Board of Governors from 1995 to June 2012, was Vice-Chair of the Board from 2005 to 2011 and Chair of the Real Estate Planning Committee from 1996 to 2012.\n\nMr. Wener is Chairman and CEO of Canderel. A native Montrealer who graduated from Concordia University in 1971 with a Bachelor of Commerce degree, Mr. Wener turned his passion for buildings and architecture into 40 years of acquiring, developing and managing office and industrial properties, residential condominiums and retail facilities.\nCommitted to community involvement, Mr. Wener launched Défi Corporatif Canderel in 1989, which has since raised more than $7 million for cancer research. As a personal expression of his belief in community service, for over 25 years Mr. Wener has played a leadership role in numerous organizations devoted to education, culture and health. He is also a director of several corporations.\nMr. Wener is a Member of the Order of Canada and a Member of the Conseil du Patronat du Québec.\n\xa0\n* deceased\n(1) Chancellor Emeritus\nThe Deputy Chancellor has all the powers of the Chancellor in his/her absence or inability to act. \xa0\n\xa0\nThe Board of Governors usually meets monthly from September to June, although the Chair of the Board may decide to dispense with a meeting in any given month. It conducts its business through its standing committees that bring recommendations to the Board.\nThe Governor's Handbook is designed as a tool for members of the Board of Governors.\xa0 It regroups useful information in understanding their duties as Governors and in fulfilling their mandate.
